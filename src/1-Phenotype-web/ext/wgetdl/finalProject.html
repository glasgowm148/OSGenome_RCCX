Data Cleaning

Before we start analyzing the data and try to draw conclusions about Wellesley and its departments, there needs to be some cleaning of the data so that it can be used.

Data were parsed from three data sources into tidy data frames in Pandas, one with course enrollment details and one with withdrawal information. Pandas functionalities and regular expressions were used to extract department codes and course information from course codes within this parsing process, which resulted in each row of the data frame corresponding to a distinct class section from a distinct semester. Departments were categorized into subject areas, namely humanities, social sciences, or sciences, in order to carry out the analyses necessary to answer question 2.

Final Conclusions

In general, we can see that:

  • The department with the smallest class sizes is the Swahili department
  • The department with the largest class sizes is the Peace and Justice department
  • Not only are there more humanities classes available but the science departments are growing at a faster rate!
  • The Humanities have smaller class sizes than the Social Sciences or the Sciences.
  • All 300 levels have smaller class sizes than 100 or 200 levels (in the Humanities, Social Sciences, and the Sciences)
  • Enrollments in science classes have always been higher in the fall
  • However, there are more classes that are taken in the fall semester, rather than the spring semester
  • Withdrawls have remained fairly consistant throughout the years
  • Most withrawls are from 200 level classes (not 100 or 300 levels)
  • The Mathematics department has had the most withdrawls (almost 500) since 2000!
